Privacy Policy – PregyApp

Last updated: 27 May 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how PregyApp (“we”, “our”, or “us”) collects, uses, discloses and protects your information when you use our mobile application PregyApp (the “App”). PregyApp is designed to assist healthcare professionals and pregnant women in monitoring anaemia and hypertension during pregnancy.

By using PregyApp, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

Depending on how you use the App, we may collect the following personal information:

  • Full name
  • Username and password
  • Contact details (such as phone number or email, if provided)
  • Patient identifiers (such as patient ID or medical record ID)
  • Doctor identifiers (such as doctor ID)

1.2 Health and Medical Information

Because PregyApp is a healthcare-related app, it may collect and store certain health-related data entered by doctors or patients, including but not limited to:

  • Haemoglobin and other blood indices (MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW, etc.)
  • Blood pressure readings (systolic and diastolic)
  • History of anaemia, hypertension and related conditions
  • General health information and symptoms entered in forms
  • Uploaded reports or images related to the patient’s condition

1.3 Usage Data

We may collect basic technical and usage information such as:

  • Device type and operating system
  • App version and basic performance data
  • Dates and times when the App is accessed

1.4 Location Data

PregyApp does not collect precise GPS location by default. If any location information is requested in future versions, it will be clearly disclosed and used only for healthcare or service-related purposes with your consent.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To create and manage user accounts (doctor and patient logins)
  • To store and display medical records and reports to authorized users
  • To help doctors monitor and manage anaemia and hypertension in pregnancy
  • To improve the App’s functionality, reliability and user experience
  • To respond to support requests and communicate with users when needed
  • To comply with legal or regulatory obligations, where applicable

3. Legal Basis for Processing (where applicable)

Depending on your jurisdiction, we may process your information based on one or more of the following:

  • Your consent
  • The performance of a contract (providing the App’s services)
  • Compliance with legal obligations
  • Legitimate interests such as improving healthcare workflows and app security

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal or health information. We may share information only in the following limited situations:

  • With treating healthcare professionals: Information entered for a patient may be visible to authorized doctors or clinic staff involved in that patient’s care.
  • Service providers: We may share information with trusted third-party service providers who help us host the database, provide analytics, or maintain the App. They are required to protect your data and use it only for the services they provide to us.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal requests by public authorities (e.g., court or government agency).

5. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse or alteration. These measures may include secure connections, authentication, and restricted access to databases.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal and health data for as long as it is necessary to provide the services within the App, to comply with legal obligations, or as required by healthcare providers’ professional practices. Data may be anonymized or deleted when it is no longer needed.

7. Children’s Privacy

PregyApp is intended to be used by healthcare professionals and adult patients. It is not directed at children under the age of 13. If we learn that we have unintentionally collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete such information as soon as possible.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data, such as the right to:

  • Access the information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your data, subject to legal or clinical constraints
  • Object to or restrict certain types of data processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided below.

9. Third-Party Links and Services

The App may integrate or interact with third-party services (such as hosting providers, analytics or authentication tools). These services may have their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review their policies when you use such services.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the App, legal requirements, or other operational needs. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. Continued use of the App after such changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
